Where was islam created at first?

In this world, Islam is the major world religion promulgated by the prophet Muhammad(s). It originated in the Arabian Peninsula in the 7th century CE. The story of Islam began in the city of Mecca, a bustling trade center located in the desert valleys of modern-day Saudi Arabia. According to Islamic tradition, the Prophet Muhammad received his first divine revelations in the year 610 CE, during a period of meditation in the cave of Hira. These revelations, which continued for 23 years, were eventually compiled into the Quran, the central religious text of Islam.

As the message of Islam spread, it transformed the social and political landscape of the region. From its spiritual roots in Mecca and Medina, the faith expanded rapidly across the Middle East, North Africa, and parts of Europe and Asia. This expansion wasn't just geographical; it was also intellectual. The Islamic Golden Age saw incredible advancements in science, mathematics, medicine, and philosophy. Today, the religion is followed by nearly 2 billion people globally, and its architectural legacy is visible in breathtaking structures like the Grand Mosque, characterized by its white marble domes and intricate geometric designs.
