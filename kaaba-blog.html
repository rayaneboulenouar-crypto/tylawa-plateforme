How many years old is Kaaba?

The traditional belief is that Abraham (Ibrahim) built the Kaaba around 5000 years ago. According to Islamic history, Ibrahim was ordered by Allah to build the sanctuary as a place of monotheistic worship.

Accompanied by his son Ishmael (Ismail), he constructed the foundations of what would become the most sacred site in Islam. Over the millennia, while the structure has been repaired and rebuilt several times, its spiritual significance has remained the heart of the Muslim world.

The Spiritual Heart of the World

Today, the Kaaba stands as the direction (Qibla) towards which millions of Muslims turn five times a day during their prayers. Its cubic form, draped in the black silk Kiswa, represents a point of absolute unity for the global community.

The traditional belief is that Ibrahim was ordered by Allah to build the sanctuary. This sacred task established Makkah as a sanctuary of peace. Every year, during the Hajj pilgrimage, the area around the Kaaba witnesses the "Tawaf", where believers circle the structure seven times, symbolizing the central role of God in a believer's life and the equality of all humans before their Creator.
